Arclength & Surface Area

  1. Calculate the arclength of these curves. Solutions can be found in Stewart.
    1. The curve \(y^2 = x^3\) between the points \((1,1)\) and \((4,8)\).
    2. The parabola \(y = x^2\) between the points \((0,0)\) and \((1,1)\).
  2. Calculate the surface-area of these solids. Solutions can be found in Stewart.
    1. The surface generated by rotating the curve \(y = \sqrt{4-x^2}\) for \(x\) between \(-1\) and \(1\) about the \(x\)-axis. This curve is part of a circle of radius two, and the resulting surface is a “ring”-like shape that’s the result of cutting two caps off the ends of a sphere.
    2. The cup-shaped surface generated by rotating the parabola \(y = x^2\) between \((0,0)\) and \((1,1)\) about the \(y\)-axis.

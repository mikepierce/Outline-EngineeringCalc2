Natural Logarithms

\[ \ln(x) = \int\limits_1^x \frac{1}{t}\,\mathrm{d}t \qquad \int\limits \frac{1}{x}\,\mathrm{d}x = \ln|x| + C \qquad \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}x}\ln|x| = \frac{1}{x} \]
  1. (Stewart) Using this “geometric” definition of \(\ln(x)\), show that \(\ln(2)\) must be some number between ½ and ¾.
  2. Calculate the derivatives of the following functions explicitly: \[ f(x)= \ln\left(3x^2-x\right) \qquad g(x) = \ln(\tan(x)) \] \[ h(x) = x^2\ln(x) \qquad j(x) = \left(\ln(x)\right)^5 \]
  3. Evaluate the following integrals. Having seen this pattern will be helpful later when we integrate general rational functions. \[ \int \frac{1}{x-1} \,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int \frac{3}{2x+5} \,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int \frac{A}{Rx+S} \,\mathrm{d}x \]
  4. Calculate the following integrals explicitly: \[ \int_0^{\pi/4} \tan\theta \,\mathrm{d}\theta \qquad \int_1^\mathrm{e} \frac{\ln(x)}{x} \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int \frac{x^2}{x^3-5} \,\mathrm{d}x \]
  5. (Stewart) Sketch the region enclosed between the curves \[ y = \frac{\ln(x)}{x} \qquad y = \frac{\left(\ln(x)\right)^2}{x} \] and find its area.
  6. (Stewart) Find the volume of the solid obtained by revolving the region under the curve \[y = \frac{1}{x^2+1}\] bound between \(x=0\) and \(x=3\) about the \(y\)-axis
  7. (Stewart) Prove the “limit laws”: \[ \ln(xy) = \ln(x) + \ln(y) \qquad \ln\left(x^a\right) = a\ln(x) \]

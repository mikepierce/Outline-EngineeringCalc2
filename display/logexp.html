General Log/Exp

  1. Compare the graphs of the two curves \( y = \mathrm{e}^x\) and \( y = 5^x\). Prove that \(5^x\) is really just a horizontal rescaling of \(\mathrm{e}^x.\) I.e. \(5^x = \mathrm{e}^{kx}\) for some constant \(k\).
  2. Similar to the last prompt, compare \( y = \ln(x)\) and \( y = \log_5(x)\), and recalling the base-change formula for logarithms show that \(\log_5(x)\) is really just a vertical rescaling of \(\ln(x).\) I.e. \(\log_5(x) = c\ln(x)\) for some constant \(c\).
  3. Calculate the following derivatives explicitly: \[ f(x) = \log_6(x) + 6^{x} \qquad \qquad g(x) = \log_6(13x) + 6^{17x} \] \[ h(x) = \log_6(13x)\times6^{17x} \qquad \qquad k(x) = \log_{10}(\sin(x)) \] \[ j(x) = 7^{\csc(x)} \qquad \qquad \ell(\theta) = \log_{42}\left(7^{\tan\theta}\right) \]
  4. Calculate the following integrals explicitly: \[ \int \frac{7}{6x+5} \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int t^7 + 7^t + 7^7 \,\mathrm{d}t \] \[ \int 3^{\cos(x)}\sin(x) \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int \pi^x\tan\left(\pi^x\right) \,\mathrm{d}x \]
  5. (Stewart) Find the area of the region bound between the curves \(y = 2^x\) and \(y = 5^x\) between \(x = -1\) and \(x=1\).
  6. Calculate the following derivatives explicitly: \[ f(x) = \sqrt{x}^x \qquad h(x) = x^{\sqrt{x}} \qquad g(x) = \sin(x)^{\cos(x)} \]
  7. (Stewart) Find \(\dot y\) if \(x^y = y^x\).

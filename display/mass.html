Centers of Mass

  1. There are two weights, one with mass 4g and another with mass 9g fastened to the ends of a 10m massless rod. Where is the rod’s fulcrum?
  2. Consider a thin straight rod aligned along the \(x\)-axis with left-most endpoint at \(x=1\) and right-most endpoint at \(x=11\).
    1. If the rod has uniform density \(\rho\), where is the rod’s fulcrum?

    2. Theorem of Pappus

      What is the area of the region generated by rotating the rod about the point \(x=0\)?
    3. If the rod has density \(\rho(x) = 1+x^2\) measured \(x\) meters from the end of the rod, where is the rod’s fulcrum?
  3. There are three weights, one with mass 4g, another with mass 9g, and another with mass 8g, fastened to the corners of a massless triangle. Choosing a coordinate system (with units m) you could describe the corners of the triangle as being located at \((2,4)\) and \((7,1)\) and \((7,5)\) respectively. Where is the triangle’s center of mass?
  4. Consider a thin triangular plate (lamina). Choosing a certain coordinate system (with units m), the corners of the triangle are located at \((2,4)\) and \((7,1)\) and \((7,5)\).
    1. If the plate has uniform density \(\rho\), where is the plate’s center of mass (centroid)?

    2. Theorem of Pappus

      What is the volume of the solid generated by rotating this triangle about the line \(x = 0\)?
    3. If the plate has density \(\rho(x) = 1+x^2\) measured \(x\) meters from its left side, where is the plate’s center of mass?
    4. If the plate has density \(\rho(x,y) = 1+xy\) at coordinates \((x,y)\), where is the plate’s center of mass?
  1. (Solution in Stewart) Find the moments and center of mass of the system of three objects having mass 3g, 4g, and 8g, each located at the coordinates \((-1,1)\), \((2,-1)\), and \((3,2)\) respectively.
  2. (Solution in Stewart) Find the center of mass of a semicircular plate of radius \(r\) with uniform density.
  3. (Solution in Stewart) Find the centroid of the region in the first quadrant bounded by the curves \(y = \cos(x)\), \(y=0\), and \(x=0\).
  4. (Solution in Stewart) Find the centroid of the region bounded by the line \(y=x\) and the parabola \(y=x^2\).

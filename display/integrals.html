Integrals

  1. Evaluate the following integrals. \[ \int\frac{1}{x^2+1}\,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int\frac{x}{x^2+1}\,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int\frac{x^2}{x^2+1}\,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int\frac{x^3}{x^2+1}\,\mathrm{d}x \]
  2. Evaluate the following integrals. \[ \int\frac{\tan(x)}{\cos(x)}\,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int \tan(x) \,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int \tan(x)\cos(x) \,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int \tan(x)\cos^2(x)\,\mathrm{d}x \]
  3. Evaluate the following integrals. \[ \int\frac{\ln(x)}{x}\,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int\ln(x)\,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int x\ln(x) \,\mathrm{d}x \]
  4. (Spivak) Evaluate the following integrals. \[ \int \frac{\arctan(x)}{1+x^2} \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int \frac{x\arctan(x)}{(1+x^2)^3} \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int \ln\big(\sqrt{1+x^2}\big) \,\mathrm{d}x \] \[ \int x\ln\big(\sqrt{1+x^2}\big) \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int \frac{x^2-1}{x^2+1}\cdot\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+x^4}} \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int \arcsin\big(\sqrt{x}\big) \,\mathrm{d}x \] \[ \int \frac{x}{1+\sin(x)} \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int \mathrm{e}^{\sin{x}} \cdot \frac{x\cos^3(x)-\sin(x)}{\cos^2(x)} \,\mathrm{d}x \] \[ \int \sqrt{\tan(x)} \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int \frac{1}{x^6+1} \,\mathrm{d}x \]

Challenges

  1. (Stewart) Find the interval \([a,b]\) for which the value of the integral \(\int_a^b (2 + x - x^2) \,\mathrm{d}x\) is a maximum.
  2. (Stewart) If \(f\) is a differentiable function such that \(\int_0^x f(t) \,\mathrm{d}t = \big(f(x)\big)^2\) for all \(x\), find a formula for \(f\).
  3. (Stewart) The figure shows a region consisting of all points inside a square that are closer to the center than to the sides of the square. Find the area of the region.
  4. (Stewart) Evaluate \(\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x^7-x}\). The straightforward approach would be to start with partial fractions, but that would be brutal. Try a substitution.

  5. The \(x \leftrightarrow \frac 1 x\) Trick

    Show that the integral \[ \int\limits_{0}^{\infty} \frac{\ln(x)}{1+x^2} \,\mathrm{d}x \] evaluates to zero by breaking up its domain of integration into two parts based on where the integrand is negative or positive, and using the substitution \(x \leftrightarrow \frac{1}{x}\) on one of those parts. Then use this same trick to show that the value of the following integral doesn't depend at all on the value of the real number \(p\). \[ \int\limits_{0}^{\pi/2} \frac{\mathrm{d}\theta}{1+\big(\tan(\theta)\big)^p} \] See this MathSE post for some reading on this trick.
  6. (Stewart) Evaluate \(\displaystyle\int_0^1 \sqrt[3]{1-x^7} - \sqrt[7]{1-x^3} \,\mathrm{d}x\,.\)
  7. (Stewart) If \(n\) is a positive integer, prove that \[\int\limits_0^1 \big(\ln(x)\big)^n \,\mathrm{d}x = (-1)^n n!\,.\]
  8. Evaluate these integrals. \[ \int \frac{1}{x^5+1} \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int \sqrt{\tan(x)} \,\mathrm{d}x \] \[ \int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sqrt{1+\mathrm{e}^{2x}}} \qquad \int \ln\left(\mu + \sqrt{1-\mu}\right)\,\mathrm{d}\mu \] \[ \int \frac{\mathrm{d}\theta}{1-\sin^4(\theta)} \]

  9. Putnam

    Evaluate these integrals. \[ \int\limits_0^1 \frac{\ln(x+1)}{1+x^2} \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int\limits_2^4 \frac{\sqrt{\ln(9-x)}}{\sqrt{\ln(9-x)}+\sqrt{\ln(x+3)}} \,\mathrm{d}x \]

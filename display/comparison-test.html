Comparison Test for Series

Examples from Stewart

  1. Determine whether this series converges or diverges: \[\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{5}{2n^2+4n+3}\]
  2. Show that \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{\ln(n)}{n}\) diverges by direct comparison.
  3. Determine whether this series converges or diverges: \[\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{2n^2+3n}{\sqrt{5+n^5}}\]

Challenges

  1. Show that this series diverges. \[\sum_{n=2}^\infty \frac{1}{\big(\ln(n)\big)^{\ln\left(\ln(n)\right)}}\] Hint: Recall that \(x^r = \mathrm{e}^{r\ln(x)}\) and that \(\ln(x) \lt \sqrt{x}\) for \(x \gt 1\).

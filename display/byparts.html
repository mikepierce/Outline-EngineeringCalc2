Integration by Parts

  1. Evaluate the following integrals by-parts. Solutions to each of them can be found in Stewart. \[ \int x \sin(x) \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int t^2 \mathrm{e}^t \,\mathrm{d}t \] \[ \int\limits_0^1 \mathrm{arctan}(x) \,\mathrm{d}x \quad \Big( \text{Hint: it's like\(\int\ln(x)\)} \Big) \]
  2. Evaluate the following integrals by-parts. \[ \int \mathrm{e}^x\cos(x) \,\mathrm{d}x \qquad \int x^5\cos(x) \,\mathrm{d}x \]
  3. Look over each of the following integrals and, without writing anything yet, try to decide if doing a substitution or if taking the integral by-parts is a better first idea. Then evaluate each integral. \[ \int x\cos\left(x^2\right) \,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int x^2\cos\left(x^2\right) \,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int \sin(x)\cos(x) \,\mathrm{d}x \] \[ \int x\ln(x) \,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int \frac{1}{x}\cos\left(\ln(x)\right) \,\mathrm{d}x \quad \int \cos\left(\ln(x)\right) \,\mathrm{d}x \]

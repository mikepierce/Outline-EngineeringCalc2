Parametrically-Defined Curves

  1. Consider the curve segment defined for \(t\) between \(0\) and \(\pi\) by the coordinates: \[x(t) = \sin(t) \quad\text{and}\quad y(t) = \sin(t)\cos(t)\]
    1. Confirm that the initial point and terminal point of this curve segment are the same point, making it a closed curve.
    2. What is an equation for the line tangent to the curve at the point where \(t = \pi/3\)?
    3. Let \(\mathrm{R}\) be the region enclosed by this curve. What is the area of \(\mathrm{R}\)?
    4. Write down an integral that computes the length of this curve.
    5. What is the surface area of the solid generated by revolving \(\mathrm{R}\) about the \(x\)-axis?

Examples from Stewart

  1. Sketch the cycloid curve defined parametrically by \[x(\theta) = 7(\theta-\sin(\theta)) \quad\text{and}\quad y(\theta) = 7(1-\cos(\theta))\]
    1. Calculate the slope of the line tangent to the cycloid at the point where \(\theta = \pi/3\).
    2. At what points on the cycloid is the tangent line perfectly horizontal? At what points on the cycloid is the tangent line perfectly vertical?
    3. Calculate the area of the region under one “arch” of the cycloid
    4. Calculate the length of a single “arch” of the cycloid.

Differential Equations

  1. Consider the differential equation \(xy'' + y' = 0\).
    1. Show that \(y = \ln(x)\) is a particular solution to this differential equation.
    2. Show that \(y = 5\ln(x) + 2\) is also a particular solution to this differential equation.
    3. Show that \(y = C_1\ln(x) + C_2\) is a general solution to this differential equation.
    4. What is the particular solution to this differential equation corresponding to initial conditions \(y(1) = 3\) and \(y(\mathrm{e}) = 5\)?
  2. Consider the differential equation \(\dot{y} = t^2 + 2\cos(t)\).
    1. What is the general solution to this differential equation?
    2. What is the particular solution to this differential equation corresponding to the initial condition \(y\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right) = \frac{\pi^3}{24}\)?
    3. An adult is riding their tricycle along the straight road that passes by their house. Their velocity \(\dot y\) (mph) can be modelled by the differential equation \(\dot{y} = t^2 + 2\cos(t)\) as a function of \(t\) hours since they left their house. If the tricyclist was \(\frac{\pi^3}{24}\) miles from their house \(\frac{\pi}{2}\) hours after they left, how far will the tricyclist be from their home in \(2\pi\) hours?

Examples from Stewart

  1. Determine whether the function \(y = x + \frac 1 x\) is a solution to either of the following differential equations. \[ x\dot{y} + y = 2x \qquad x\ddot{y} + 2\dot{y} = 0 \]
  2. Show that every member of the family of functions \[y = \frac{1+c\mathrm{e}^t}{1-c\mathrm{e}^t}\] is a solution to the differential equation \(\dot{y} = \frac{1}{2}\left(y^2-1\right)\).
  3. Find a solution to the differential equation \(\dot{y} = \frac{1}{2}\left(y^2-1\right)\) that satisfies the initial condition \(y(0)=2\).

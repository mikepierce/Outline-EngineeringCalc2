  5. The \(x \leftrightarrow \frac 1 x\) Trick

    Show that the integral \[ \int\limits_{0}^{\infty} \frac{\ln(x)}{1+x^2} \,\mathrm{d}x \] evaluates to zero by breaking up its domain of integration into two parts based on where the integrand is negative or positive, and using the substitution \(x \leftrightarrow \frac{1}{x}\) on one of those parts. Then use this same trick to show that the value of the following integral doesn't depend at all on the value of the real number \(p\). \[ \int\limits_{0}^{\pi/2} \frac{\mathrm{d}\theta}{1+\big(\tan(\theta)\big)^p} \] See this MathSE post for some reading on this trick.
